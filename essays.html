Essays

Someone recently expressed anger with me because they wanted to know where is my outrage.

Where is my outrage? I’ll tell you about my outrage.

It’s in the 33% of women who experience domestic abuse, stalking, rape, and murder and the men who perpetrate known and unknown travesties upon them.

It’s in the adults who perpetrate evil upon children through sex and human trafficking.
It’s in the 672,000 children in foster care and 52M orphans in Africa.
It’s in a political system that thinks it’s OK to kill babies ~900,000 a year.

It’s in the governmental leaders who allow thousands of mass protests all over the country but don’t protect the rights of church members who want to congregate.
It’s in the same leaders who think people get Covid from restaurants but don’t monitor parks and frat parties and all the other super-spreader events.

It’s in the tax and spend governmental bodies who pile mountains of laws and regulations and taxes on small businesses and don’t take responsibility or express remorse when these small businesses are sued by citizens so offended at minor “injustices”, causing businesses to spend money fighting frivolous lawsuits year after year.
It’s in the decimation of small businesses that borrowed 100s of thousands of dollars to keep their businesses running through a pandemic while state and city leaders tightened restrictions on them in ways that made it impossible to stay afloat.

It’s in the cancel culture taking over this country. Civil political discourse is not allowed. Respectful disagreement is considered racist and inflammatory commentary. It’s in the mind blowing media “experts” who slant all stories to benefit themselves or their personal interests.

It’s in the people who don’t know others well because they don’t take the time to live with and sit with and listen to each other instead of condemning and maligning their character.

I’ve spent 25+ years of service in the fight for justice and truth. I don’t appreciate being lambasted for not being “outraged” like you.

I still love you,
Jennifer
